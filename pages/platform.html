Economy

We believe in the free market economy. Every American has the right to own and prosper, which is taken away when the federal government controls the industry. Economics and corporate practices should prove themselves, rather than the government choosing who thrive. We also support a trickle down economy. We stand by the idea that benefits for corporations and the wealthy will positively affect everyone else.

Safety

We believe in free regulations regarding firearms in order to ensure the security of the citizens. It is up to the invidiual to exercise the responsibility that comes with having this freedom. American citizens should generally have the right to own, carry, and use guns to protect themselves. Any kind of government regulation on gun rights goes against the Constitution.

Religion

We strongly support freedom of religion. The inalienable rights and liberties we have right now are derived from God and these rights of individuals, businesses, and organizations are prohibited from being violated.

Healthcare

We strive for a patient-centered healthcare system. This will be based on the free market so that healthcare costs will go down. This is the best solution because it will ensure an increase in satisfaction rates in both the patient and their families. It will also benefit the patient-physician relationship because the reputations for the providers will increase with a rise in patient satisfaction. This will prevent a decrease in efficiency and increased waiting periods, which is evident in a government-centered healthcare system such as Obamacare.

Terrorism

We believe that strong and quick actions must be taken in response to terrorist attacks. We do not seek war, and in order to prevent this, we must limit the production of weapons of mass destruction and take on a more offensive front. If needed, military forces must be used.

Energy

Our goal is to produce clean, affordable, secure, and abundant energy within America and reduce vulnerability to energy price volatility. We can know how to best use oil, coal, natural gas, and other natural resources once we know how much we actually have, which is why opening up public lands and the outer continental shelf is necessary for planning energy use. Coal in particular should be mined in order to ensure the jobs of lower income Americans. We have been limited by government regulations for too long, but they should be removed for our nation to truly prosper.
